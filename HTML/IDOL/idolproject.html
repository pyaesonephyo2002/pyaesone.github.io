Early life and Education

Jennie Kim, known professionally as Jennie, is a South Korean singer and rapper, born on January 16, 1996, in Seoul, South Korea. She gained fame as a member of the K-pop girl group BLACKPINK, which debuted under YG Entertainment in 2016. Jennie spent much of her early life in New Zealand, where she attended ACG Parnell College. She later returned to South Korea to pursue her dreams of becoming a singer. Before her debut with BLACKPINK, Jennie underwent several years of training under YG Entertainment, honing her skills in singing, dancing, and rapping.Since BLACKPINK's debut, Jennie has become well-known for her versatile talents, captivating stage presence, and distinctive fashion sense, making her one of the prominent figures in the global K-pop phenomenon.

Carrer

Jennie debuted as a member of BLACKPINK under YG Entertainment in August 2016 with their single album "Square One," which included the hits "Boombayah" and "Whistle." The group quickly gained attention for their powerful performances and distinctive style. Since their debut, BLACKPINK has become one of the most successful K-pop girl groups globally. They have released numerous hit songs such as "Playing with Fire," "As If It's Your Last," "DDU-DU DDU-DU," and "Kill This Love," which have topped charts both in South Korea and internationally. In addition to her activities with BLACKPINK, Jennie has pursued solo projects. She released her debut solo single "Solo" in November 2018, which was well-received and showcased her versatility as a performer.
